ZEUS

God of the sky, the lightning, the thunder, the law, the order, and the justice. Zeus is the child of Cronus and Rhea, was the king of the gods, the ruler of Mount Olympus, married to Hera, and the god of the sky and thunder in Greek mythology. His symbols are the thunderbolt, eagle, bull, and oak.

POSEIDON

God of the sea, earthquakes, and horses. Poseidon, child of Cronus & Rhea, was the god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses in Greek myths. He's one of the twelve Olympian gods who resided on Mount Olympus. Poseidon symbols include the trident, fish, and dolphin.

HERA

queen of the twelve Olympians and Mount Olympus, goddess of marriage, women, childbirth, and family. Hera was the wife and one of three sisters of Zeus in the Olympian pantheon of Greek mythology. Her symbols include the peacock, cow, cuckoo, and pomegranate.

HERMES

Messenger of the gods, god of trade, thieves, travelers, sports, and guide to the underworld. Hermes is considered the herald of the gods and the protector of human heralds, travelers, thieves, merchants and orators. His symbols include the caduceus, the winged sandals, and the winged helmet.

ATHENA

Goddess of wisdom, courage, inspiration, civilization, law and justice, strategic warfare, mathematics, strength, strategy, the arts, crafts, and skill. Athena is known for her calm temperament and compassionate heart. She is the patron goddess of heroic endeavor and one of the goddesses who protected Athens.

ARES

God of war, bloodshed, and violence. Ares was the Greek god of war and one of the twelve Olympian gods. In literature, Ares is generally portrayed as the son of Zeus and Hera, but no ancient authors mention this relationship. His symbols include the sword, spear, shield, and dog.

HEPHAESTUS

God of fire, metalworking, stone masonry, forges, and the art of sculpture. Hephaestus was the Greek god of blacksmiths, craftsmen, artisans, sculptors, metals, metallurgy, fire, and volcanoes. His symbols include the hammer, anvil, tongs, and volcano.

APHRODITE

Goddess of love, beauty, pleasure, and procreation. Aphrodite was the Olympian goddess of love, beauty, pleasure, and procreation. She was married to Hephaestus, the god of blacksmiths.

HADES

God of the dead, the Underworld, and riches. Hades was the ancient Greek god of the dead and the ruler of the Underworld. He was one of the three sons of Cronus and Rhea, and he spent most of his time in the Underworld, away from the affairs of gods and men.

PONTUS

Primordial god of the sea, father of the fish and other sea creatures. Pontus was the ancient Greek god of the sea and the primordial deity of the ocean. He was one of the first beings to emerge from Chaos, the void that existed before the creation of the universe.

DIONYSUS

God of wine, winemaking, fertility. Dionysus was the ancient Greek god of wine, winemaking, grape-harvest, fertility, ritual madness, religious ecstasy, and theater. He was also known as Bacchus by the Greeks for a frenzy he is said to drink called baccheia.

IRIS

Personification of the rainbow and messenger of the gods. In Greek mythology, Iris is the personification of the rainbow and the messenger of the gods. She is also known as one of the goddesses of the sea and the sky.
